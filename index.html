ℹ️ Dashboard Summary & Architecture

What it does: Macro-level forecasting, team composition, and handle-time resource planning.

Core Focus: This dashboard answers the question: “How many people do we need on staff to process our intake volume and meet our enrollment goals?” It bridges your marketing numbers with your physical roster limitations.

Key Features

  • Models handle times for Phone Screens (PS) vs. Evaluations (EV).
  • Calculates the split of upper-funnel triage between stateless global pools (PFAs) and state-licensed evaluators (CEs).
  • Computes "Ideal Need" vs. "Strict Need" to tell you the literal headcount needed to survive calendar restrictions, shrinkage, and step-by-step conversion drops.
  • Bottom-Up Math: It locks in your net available hours (after stripping out granular CE and PFA PTO) and slides that raw capacity down the funnel to project your true mathematical maximum throughput.

🧳 The Suitcase Paradox Analogy

Strict Quota: The CE's suitcase has a rigid foam insert with exactly 4 Phone Screen slots and 3 Eval slots. If there isn't enough Phone Screen demand to fill the 4 slots, those slots just stay empty. The CE is maxed out at 3 Evals, so their Eval Utilization looks extremely high.

Fluid Time: The suitcase is just an empty duffel bag containing 5 hours of total space. If the CE only packs 1 hour worth of Phone Screens, they can pack 4 hours worth of Evals. Because they have so much extra room for Evals, their Eval Utilization percentage drops safely into the green.
